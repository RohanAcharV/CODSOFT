    Biography


    Puneeth Rajkumar, affectionately known as Appu, was a versatile and beloved figure in the world of Indian entertainment, particularly renowned for his contributions to Kannada cinema. Born on March 17, 1975, he hailed from a distinguished film lineage as the youngest son of the iconic actor Dr. Rajkumar, which naturally placed great expectations upon him. Puneeth's career spanned over three decades, during which he showcased exceptional talent and versatility.

    His journey in the film industry began as a child artist, where his performances in films such as "Bettada Hoovu" garnered critical acclaim and earned him the prestigious National Film Award for Best Child Artist. Puneeth's early success foreshadowed a career filled with accolades and adoration.

    As he transitioned into lead roles, Puneeth continued to captivate audiences with his magnetic presence on the silver screen. His filmography included a wide range of genres, from family dramas to action-packed blockbusters. Some of his notable films, such as "Raajakumara ", "Yuvarathnaa" ,"Milana", "Jackie", "Hudugaru" , "Power" not only entertained but also conveyed important social messages. These films solidified his status as one of Kannada cinema's most celebrated actors.

    Beyond his acting prowess, Puneeth ventured into playback singing, following in the footsteps of his father. He used his singing talents not only for artistic expression but also as a means to contribute to charitable causes. Puneeth's commitment to giving back to society was evident throughout his life. Puneeth Rajkumar's impact extended far beyond the entertainment industry. His philanthropic endeavors, including a significant eye donation campaign, left an enduring legacy. Even after his untimely passing on October 29, 2021, Puneeth's contributions continued to inspire and uplift countless lives. Recognizing his exceptional contributions, the Karnataka Government posthumously honored him with Karnataka Ratna, the state's highest civilian award, on November 1, 2022.     Popularity and Other works


    Puneeth Rajkumar, a renowned actor in the Kannada film industry, left a lasting legacy of philanthropy and social commitment. He served as the brand ambassador for the Bangalore Metropolitan Transport Corporation (BMTC) in 2019, promoting public transportation without seeking any financial compensation. Emulating his father's footsteps, Puneeth endorsed Karnataka Milk Federation (KMF) products for a decade, emphasizing his refusal to accept payment for the role. He actively supported education initiatives, including the Right to Education (RTE) program, sponsoring students' fees, and advocating for reduced school drop-out rates. During the COVID-19 pandemic, Puneeth contributed significantly to relief efforts and urged his fans to do the same. Beyond his acting career, he engaged in philanthropic work and owned the Bengaluru Royals, a Premier Futsal team. Puneeth was not only a talented actor but also a gifted playback singer, donating his singing earnings to charity and orphanages. He also ventured into television hosting and production. Puneeth was a sought-after brand ambassador for various products and brands. Additionally, he founded and owned the music label PRK Audio, which garnered immense popularity on YouTube, boasting over 15.1 lakh subscribers as of November 1, 2022. Puneeth Rajkumar's multifaceted contributions and unwavering commitment to social causes continue to inspire many.

    Awards and Honors


    • He won the National Film Award for Best Child Artist for his role of Ramu in Bettada Hoovu.
    • He also won Karnataka State Award Best Child artist for Chalisuva Modagalu and Eradu Nakshatragalu.
    • In a career spanning three decades, he has won one National Film Award, four Karnataka State Film Awards, six Filmfare Awards South, and five SIIMA awards.
    • He was conferred with the Doctorate by Mysuru University.
    • The Karnataka Government conferred the state's highest civilian award, Karnataka Ratna, to Puneeth Rajkumar on 1 November 2022, posthumously.
    • The State Government announced to mark his birthday as "Inspiration Day".
